# DAARION MicroDAO Pricing Recommendation

Status: product/business recommendation

Date: 2026-06-19

Owner repo for user-facing product journey: `loval-echoes`

Scope: docs-only. No billing implementation, payment integration, smart
contract change, frontend change, backend change, Edge Client change, Local
Agent Runtime work, or Worker Node work is included.

## Final Classification

```text
PRICING SHOULD BE RESTRUCTURED
```

## Executive Recommendation

DAARION should move from one public `2 DAAR/month` Leader price to a staged
pricing ladder:

| Stage | Plan | Monthly price | Intended customer | Limit |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Founding launch | Founding MicroDAO | `2 DAAR` | First serious early adopters | First 10 MicroDAOs, permanent founding price |
| Public starter | Starter | `5 DAAR` | Small teams and communities | Up to 10 members |
| Public community | Community | `15 DAAR` | Growing communities | Up to 25 members |
| Public core | Leader | `30 DAAR` | Operational MicroDAO leaders | Up to 50 members |
| Later expansion | Organization | `100+ DAAR` | Multi-community or institution-scale users | Custom |

The current `2 DAAR/month` price should remain available only as a limited
founding price for the first 10 MicroDAOs. For that cohort, the founding price
should remain permanent as long as the MicroDAO remains active and compliant
with product policy. It should not be marketed as the normal full Leader price
for a 50-member MicroDAO.

## Why This Model Fits DAARION

DAARION is not selling only chat.

The product bundle includes or is preparing:

- MicroDAO workspace;
- Community Spirit AI;
- memory/RAG;
- chats;
- tasks;
- knowledge base;
- member invitations;
- Connect Device bridge;
- future local device/runtime support;
- future active agent and Worker Node paths.

A sustainable model should price the MicroDAO as an operating space with an AI
layer, not as a low-cost assistant subscription.

## Recommended Launch Pricing

### 1. Founding MicroDAO

```text
2 DAAR / month
First 10 MicroDAOs
```

Purpose:

- create urgency;
- reward first serious users;
- collect product feedback;
- keep initial onboarding attractive;
- avoid locking public pricing too low while still creating real scarcity.

Recommended terms:

- manually approved;
- limited to first 10 MicroDAOs;
- permanent founding price for those first 10 MicroDAOs;
- 1 active MicroDAO;
- up to 50 members only if approved by team;
- one Community Spirit Agent;
- basic memory/RAG;
- early access to Connect Device once backend status is real;
- no guarantee of Worker Node eligibility;
- non-transferable without manual approval.

Recommended copy:

```text
Founding MicroDAO
2 DAAR / month
For the first 10 approved MicroDAO leaders.
```

Important:

Do not call this the normal Leader Plan after public pricing is introduced.

### 2. Starter

```text
5 DAAR / month
Up to 10 members
```

Purpose:

- simple public entry price;
- viable for small communities;
- protects the platform from 50-member usage at founding price;
- gives early users an upgrade path before full Leader.

Recommended included value:

- 1 active MicroDAO;
- up to 10 members;
- 1 Community Spirit Agent;
- basic memory/RAG quota;
- chats and tasks;
- knowledge base with conservative storage limits;
- member invitations up to plan limit;
- 1 connected device after device pairing/status is implemented;
- fair-use AI budget.

Recommended copy:

```text
Starter
5 DAAR / month
For small MicroDAOs preparing their first operating rhythm.
```

### 3. Community

```text
15 DAAR / month
Up to 25 members
```

Purpose:

- create a natural growth step between Starter and Leader;
- price the extra load from more chats, memory, agent use, and moderation;
- avoid forcing users to jump from `5 DAAR` directly to `30 DAAR`;
- keep per-member economics consistent before the 50-member tier.

Recommended included value:

- 1 active MicroDAO;
- up to 25 members;
- 1 Community Spirit Agent;
- medium AI fair-use budget;
- medium memory/RAG quota;
- chats, tasks, knowledge base, and basic reports;
- member invitations up to plan limit;
- 2 to 3 connected devices after device pairing/status is implemented;
- no Worker Node guarantee.

Recommended copy:

```text
Community
15 DAAR / month
For growing MicroDAOs coordinating up to 25 members.
```

At the repo's current implied rate of `1 DAAR = 10 USD`, this is `150 USD/month`.
At 25 members, that is `6 USD/member/month`.

### 4. Leader

```text
30 DAAR / month
Up to 50 members
```

Purpose:

- the primary sustainable paid plan;
- prices a MicroDAO as an operational community workspace;
- creates margin for AI, memory, support, and future device features.

Recommended included value:

- 1 active MicroDAO;
- up to 50 members;
- 1 Community Spirit Agent;
- higher AI fair-use budget;
- larger memory/RAG quota;
- chats, tasks, knowledge base, and reports;
- member invitations up to plan limit;
- 3 to 5 connected devices after device pairing/status is implemented;
- priority access to device readiness and agent capability previews;
- no Worker Node guarantee.

Recommended copy:

```text
Leader
30 DAAR / month
For active MicroDAO leaders coordinating up to 50 members.
```

At the repo's current implied rate of `1 DAAR = 10 USD`, this is `300 USD/month`.
At 50 members, that is `6 USD/member/month`, which is a more defensible price
for an AI-assisted operating workspace than the current `20 USD/month` flat
price. It also keeps the same approximate per-member economics as the
25-member Community plan.

### 5. Organization

```text
100+ DAAR / month
Custom
```

Purpose:

- avoid forcing institutions or multi-community networks into the same 50-member
  plan;
- create a path for DAARION.city-scale deployments;
- protect support, security, device, and agent workload boundaries.

Recommended included value:

- multiple MicroDAOs or larger member limits;
- custom storage/memory;
- custom agent usage;
- custom device count;
- support and onboarding;
- governance or enterprise requirements only when explicitly implemented;
- Worker Node relationship handled through a separate operator program.

Recommended copy:

```text
Organization
100+ DAAR / month
For networks that need multiple MicroDAOs, larger limits, or dedicated support.
```

## Launch Policy

Recommended launch policy:

1. Keep the current `2 DAAR/month` price only for the first 10 approved
   Founding MicroDAOs.
2. Treat that Founding price as permanent for those first 10 MicroDAOs while
   they remain active and policy-compliant.
3. Publicly introduce Starter, Community, and Leader after the authenticated
   Dashboard -> Connect Device -> install handoff is validated.
4. Do not require device readiness for Dashboard access.
5. Do not bundle Worker Node rights into MicroDAO plans.
6. Do not expose raw token/LLM accounting to normal users at launch.
7. Do define internal fair-use limits for AI, storage, and device status.

## Founder Pricing Policy

Founding pricing should answer three questions before implementation:

| Question | Recommendation |
| --- | --- |
| Is it permanent? | Yes. The first 10 approved MicroDAOs keep `2 DAAR/month` permanently while active and policy-compliant. |
| Is it transferable? | No, not without manual approval. |
| Does it include future Worker Node? | No. Worker Node remains separate. |

Recommended founder language:

```text
Founding pricing is a permanent early access privilege for the first 10 approved
MicroDAO leaders. It covers the MicroDAO workspace and early product feedback
loop while the MicroDAO remains active and policy-compliant. It does not
automatically include future operator, Worker Node, or enterprise features.
```

## Long-Term Pricing Direction

Long-term pricing should combine:

- plan tier by member count;
- included AI budget;
- included storage/RAG quota;
- included active agent count;
- included connected device count;
- add-ons for additional usage;
- separate operator pricing for Worker Nodes.

Recommended long-term structure:

```text
Plan price
+ extra active agents
+ extra storage/memory
+ extra connected devices
+ high-usage AI packs
+ optional support/onboarding
```

Do not introduce this complexity in the first public pricing UI. It should be
documented internally and activated when usage data proves the need.

## What Should Not Be Priced Yet

Do not price these as normal public plan features until their contracts and
product paths are implemented:

- backend-backed device pairing invitation;
- authoritative Dashboard device status;
- Local Agent Runtime;
- Worker Node participation;
- governance signing;
- marketplace or federation;
- agent routing beyond the current Community Spirit scope.

These should remain roadmap or advanced-access items, not included promises in
the public paid plan.

## Minimum Implementation After This Docs PR

This PR does not change implementation. The smallest safe implementation path
after business approval is:

1. Add plan definitions for `founding`, `starter`, `community`, `leader`, and
   later `organization`.
2. Keep current `leader` config untouched until a migration/copy PR is approved.
3. Add database-backed plan config rows only after pricing is approved.
4. Update pricing copy to replace "unlimited participant invitations" with
   "member invitations up to your plan limit".
5. Update billing UI to display the selected plan and member limit.
6. Keep testing-mode onboarding separate from paid plan enforcement until the
   product journey is validated.
