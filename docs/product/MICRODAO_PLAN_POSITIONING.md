# DAARION MicroDAO Plan Positioning

Status: product positioning recommendation

Date: 2026-06-19

Owner repo for user-facing product journey: `loval-echoes`

Scope: docs-only positioning. No pricing change, billing implementation,
payment integration, smart contract change, frontend change, backend change,
Edge Client change, Local Agent Runtime work, or Worker Node work is included.

## Positioning Goal

Explain why each DAARION MicroDAO plan costs money in outcome language.

The pricing ladder is already defined. This document does not change prices.
It defines the message that should sit behind future pricing UI and billing
copy.

## Core Product Promise

Recommended one-sentence promise:

```text
DAARION MicroDAO turns a group of people into an operating community with shared
memory, clear tasks, AI-assisted coordination, and a path to connected devices.
```

Shorter public version:

```text
Run your community with memory, coordination, and an AI helper that keeps the
work moving.
```

## What A User Buys

A user is not buying "RAG" or "one AI assistant" as isolated features.

A user is buying:

- less repeated explanation;
- fewer lost decisions;
- fewer forgotten tasks;
- faster onboarding of new members;
- one place for community knowledge and work;
- an AI helper that understands the community context;
- a gradual path from web workspace to connected device and local runtime.

## Outcome Ladder

| Plan | Outcome statement |
| --- | --- |
| Founding | Lock early access and build one of the first DAARION MicroDAOs with permanent founding pricing. |
| Starter | Move a small group from scattered chat into one shared operating rhythm. |
| Community | Keep a growing community aligned as more members, memory, and coordination load appear. |
| Leader | Operate a serious 50-member MicroDAO with persistent memory, accountability, and AI-assisted coordination. |
| Organization | Coordinate multiple or larger communities with custom limits, support, and future infrastructure options. |

## Recommended Plan Positioning

### Founding

Price:

```text
2 DAAR / month
First 10 approved MicroDAOs
Permanent founding price while active and policy-compliant
```

Audience:

```text
Early leaders who want to help shape DAARION and lock scarce first-cohort
access.
```

Outcome:

```text
Start one of the first DAARION MicroDAOs and keep the founding price while your
community grows.
```

Recommended description:

```text
For the first 10 approved MicroDAO leaders who want to build early, give
feedback, and keep permanent founding pricing.
```

Recommended bullets:

- Launch one active MicroDAO.
- Get the Community Spirit helper for early coordination.
- Start preserving decisions, tasks, and community knowledge.
- Receive early access to device connection when the backend path is ready.
- Keep the founding price while the MicroDAO remains active and compliant.

Avoid:

- implying investor rights;
- promising Worker Node access;
- promising all future features forever;
- calling it the normal Leader plan.

### Starter

Price:

```text
5 DAAR / month
Up to 10 members
```

Audience:

```text
Small communities that need structure before coordination becomes messy.
```

Outcome:

```text
Give a small team one place for decisions, tasks, knowledge, and AI-assisted
follow-through.
```

Recommended description:

```text
For small MicroDAOs that need a shared operating rhythm instead of scattered
messages and repeated reminders.
```

Recommended bullets:

- Coordinate up to 10 members in one workspace.
- Keep agreements and knowledge available to the group.
- Turn conversations into tasks and next actions.
- Give new members a clearer place to start.
- Prepare one device connection path when that contract is active.

Avoid:

- leading with RAG terminology;
- overpromising device readiness;
- presenting it as a cheap version of Leader only.

### Community

Price:

```text
15 DAAR / month
Up to 25 members
```

Audience:

```text
Growing communities where chats, onboarding, and decisions are becoming harder
to hold manually.
```

Outcome:

```text
Keep a growing community aligned before the leader becomes the bottleneck.
```

Recommended description:

```text
For growing MicroDAOs that need stronger memory, clearer roles, and more
AI-assisted coordination as activity increases.
```

Recommended bullets:

- Coordinate up to 25 members.
- Preserve decisions and context as the group grows.
- Support more active conversations, tasks, and knowledge.
- Reduce repeated explanations for new or returning members.
- Prepare for multiple connected devices once device status is implemented.

Avoid:

- making it sound like only a member-count upgrade;
- hiding why it costs 3x Starter;
- bundling Worker Node or Local Agent Runtime before they are ready.

### Leader

Price:

```text
30 DAAR / month
Up to 50 members
```

Audience:

```text
Serious MicroDAO leaders running an active community, team, or project network.
```

Outcome:

```text
Operate a full MicroDAO with persistent memory, accountability, and AI-assisted
coordination across up to 50 members.
```

Recommended description:

```text
For active MicroDAO leaders who need the workspace to remember, coordinate, and
turn community activity into accountable work.
```

Recommended bullets:

- Coordinate up to 50 members.
- Keep community memory, agreements, and knowledge available.
- Use the Community Spirit helper to support onboarding and follow-through.
- Turn chats into tasks, summaries, and decisions.
- Prepare for more connected devices and future agent capability previews.

Avoid:

- saying "unlimited participant invitations";
- describing the price only as "AI assistant + RAG";
- implying Organization-scale support.

### Organization

Price:

```text
100+ DAAR / month
Custom
```

Audience:

```text
Networks, institutions, or operators that need larger limits, multiple
MicroDAOs, or dedicated support.
```

Outcome:

```text
Coordinate a larger DAARION operating structure with custom limits, support,
and future infrastructure options.
```

Recommended description:

```text
For organizations that need multiple MicroDAOs, larger member limits, custom
support, or a path toward private infrastructure.
```

Recommended bullets:

- Custom MicroDAO and member limits.
- Custom memory, agent, and device planning.
- Support for larger onboarding and operating requirements.
- Future infrastructure and governance options only when explicitly contracted.
- Separate Worker Node/operator path when applicable.

Avoid:

- promising self-hosting before release readiness;
- bundling governance, payments, or worker economics too early;
- making Organization look like a public self-serve tier.

## Pricing Page Message Hierarchy

Recommended future order:

1. Outcome headline.
2. One-sentence MicroDAO definition.
3. Plan ladder by community size and operating load.
4. Plan cards with outcome descriptions.
5. Feature details below each outcome.
6. Clear note that device, Local Agent, Worker Node, and Organization features
   are advanced or future paths unless already implemented.

## Feature-To-Outcome Rewrite Guide

| Avoid leading with | Prefer leading with |
| --- | --- |
| RAG memory | Your community stops losing knowledge and decisions. |
| AI assistant | A Community Spirit helper keeps context and supports follow-through. |
| Group chats | Members have one place to discuss, decide, and act. |
| Tasks | Conversations become visible work and next steps. |
| Invitations | New members can join the right workspace with less manual setup. |
| Device support | Prepare this device when local capabilities become useful. |
| Crypto billing | Pay with DAAR and supported crypto assets. |

## Success Criteria For Future UI Copy

Future pricing UI should let a new user answer:

- What is a MicroDAO?
- What operating problem does it solve?
- Why does DAARION charge monthly?
- Why does the plan price increase with member count?
- What do I get now?
- What is future/advanced and not included yet?

## Final Answer

The best plan positioning is:

```text
DAARION MicroDAO is paid because it turns community activity into an operating
system: shared memory, coordinated tasks, clearer decisions, easier onboarding,
and AI-assisted follow-through.
```

The current UI partially communicates the features, but the next copy milestone
should make the outcomes explicit before the new pricing ladder is exposed
publicly.
